Interests.

Quantum computing

The development of a successful quantum computer would have massive implications on data processing, cryptography, and computation as we know it. I am currently a user on IBM Quantum Experience, which is a wonderful tool for learning about quantum computation.

Particle physics

Fundamental particles comprise all matter that we know, and being able to understand them allows us to predict how the universe will behave -- consequently facilitating further development in science- and technology-related fields.

Network structures

From a microscopic to a macroscopic scale, a vast amount of systems, processes, and phenomena are governed by the principles associated with network structures. This article from the Wolfram blog even postulates that a network-related theory might be the elusive unified "theory of everything".

Chaos theory

It's always incredible when order emerges out of chaos which is, essentially, the phenomenon of the entire universe existing. In particular, fractals are patterns that are recursively, but still dynamically generated -- a perfect visual example of, ironically, predictability arising directly from chaos.
